# Sales Data Quality Audit & Profitability Analysis for Retail Business
  ## Executive Summary

This project analyzes retail sales data to identify key drivers of revenue and profitability. The dataset contained multiple data quality issues (missing values, inconsistent categories, incorrect entries), which were cleaned and standardized before analysis.

The findings reveal that while revenue remains stable over time, the business is largely unprofitable across most categories and regions. Losses are primarily driven by specific product categories and geographic markets. The analysis provides actionable recommendations to improve pricing strategies, optimize product focus, and enhance profitability.

## Business Problem

The company relies on sales data to monitor performance and support strategic decisions. However, inconsistencies and data quality issues reduce the reliability of insights.

Additionally, despite strong revenue performance, the company struggles with profitability. This raises key business questions:

Why is the business not profitable despite high sales?
Which products and regions are driving losses?
How can the company improve margins and decision-making?

This project aims to clean the data, uncover key performance drivers, and provide actionable business recommendations.

## Methodology

The analysis followed a structured data analytics workflow:

Data Cleaning & Validation
Handled missing values and duplicates
Standardized categorical variables (e.g., regions)
Corrected data inconsistencies (e.g., negative sales values)
Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
Time-series analysis (monthly sales trends)
Aggregation by category, region, and customer
Profitability Analysis
Computed profit margins
Identified loss-making segments
Segmentation Analysis
Category-level performance
Region-level performance
Customer-level profitability

## Results & Business Recommendations
### Key Findings
Revenue vs Profit Disconnect
High-revenue categories (Furniture, Technology) are unprofitable
Office Supplies is the only consistently profitable category
Regional Performance Issues
USA generates strong sales but the largest losses
Europe is closest to break-even
Seasonality & Volatility
Sales show seasonal peaks (March, July, October)
No strong long-term growth trend
Customer Profitability Gaps
Some high-revenue customers generate negative profit
Business Recommendations
Optimize Pricing & Costs
Review pricing strategies for Furniture and Technology
Reduce costs in loss-making segments
Focus on Profitable Segments
Scale Office Supplies, especially in Europe and Asia
Improve Regional Efficiency
Investigate operational inefficiencies in the USA market
Customer Strategy Optimization
Reassess unprofitable high-revenue customers
Leverage Seasonal Trends
Replicate successful strategies from peak months
